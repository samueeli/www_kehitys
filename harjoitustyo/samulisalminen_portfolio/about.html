About Me

Story

I was born in 1984 in Finland. I spent my childhood running around forests playing hide and seek with my friends, jumping from rock to rock and climbing trees. I wasn’t particularly interested about technology. In fact, we didn’t even have a computer at home. Back then, that was the case with most households.

The first computer course I remember was in secondary school when I was around thirteen. At that time those clumsy Microsoft Paint, Word and Netscape weren’t enough to sweep me off my feet. I was more into music and creative arts. My mom put me into music play school when I was 4 years old and later on that led me to my first profession.

My parents were open to different cultures. When an opportunity opened for me to go to Los Angeles to study music, the idea felt quite natural and exciting. In the USA I met my future wife Marianne, an exchange student from Switzerland. After finishing my studies at Los Angeles Music Academy in 2007, I moved to Switzerland and we got married. I got an internship at a local church where I was responsible for organising the music. Spirituality started to interest me. In the Fall of 2009, I began my pastoral studies at Suomen teologinen opisto.

Ok, now that you know a little bit about my history let me finally tell you how I came around IT and became a developer.

Seven years, two degrees and 3 children later, we were living in Tampere Finland. Things weren’t easy. My wife was on maternity leave and I was unemployed. I had to do something. And as uncreative and unintuitive it felt, I decided to make a rational decision and ask myself: “Which sector has the greatest potential for the future?” The answer I came up with was technology. So I grabbed every internet course I could find and started studying. In six months I landed my first developer job.

Today I’ve been working as a developer for a year and a half. It has been an incredible journey. As a creativity driven person I thought I would never truly be interested about IT because to me it seemed so uncreative. I was wrong. IT can be as creative as we let it. The world of technology is a world of endless possibilities. In here the only limit is our own imagination.
